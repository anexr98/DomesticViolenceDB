What is Domestic Violence ?









In a relationship, there are ups and downs, sometimes we say and do things to each other that are not nice. But there is a difference between normal arguing and abusive fighting behaviour in the home which is called Domestic Violence. Abuse is when one person hurts or bullies another person. Hence, Domestic Violence is when this abuse happens between two people in a family or between 2 people who are staying/living together married or have children together.

Sometimes a member of the family can be the abuse i.e (mother, brother, sister, grand parent, foster parent).

Domestic Violence normally happens to women but it can also happen to men.

Different kinds of physical abuse that can be part of domestic violence are:

Physical Abuse

  • Hitting
  • Pushing
  • Slapping

Domestic Violence is about one person thinking he/she can control the other person and can include many different types of abuse like:

Verbal Abuse

  • Shouting
  • Emotional Abuse
  • Calling them names

Laws

What does the law provide to protect you against Domestic Violence ?

  • Laws exist to protect you your children or any person living under the same roof from violence or threat of violence.
  • Domestic Violence is a criminal offence thus you can file a complaint at a police station and if you have suffered injury such as wounds and blows you have to fill a legal form PF 58.
  • Then it is required to attend the nearest hospital for a medical examination.
  • Following the complaints an inquiry will be carried out and legal proceedings will be initiated against the perpetrator of violence (abuser).
  • If you do not want to file a complaint you can always contact the police for assistance in case of emergency.
  • If youâ€™ve been abused or fear domestic violence please call on the Hot Line - 119 (Toll Free Number).

Hot Line - 119 (Toll Free Number)

  • You may call for help at the FSB closest to your place of residence.
  • A liaison officer will provide you with first hand counselling and refer you to a Family Welfare and Protection Officer.
  • In case you wish to apply for a Protection Order, you will be requested to swear an affidavit and you will be escorted by the officer to the District Magistrates Chambers where an order will be issued providing you with the protection you need.

Existing Protections

What are the existing protections under the Protection from Domestic Violence Act (PDVA)?

You can apply for the following at a District Court:

  • A Protection Order
  • An Occupation Order
  • A Tenancy Order
  • An Interim Order

    • What is a Protection Order ?

    A protection order is an order of restraint against the person it has been issued preventing him/her from continuing to adopt violent behaviour/conduct. The court can also order that perpetrator to stop harassing, contacting, intimidating and threatening the victim at his/her place of residence, place of work or any other venue.

    What is an Occupation Order ?

    It is an order of the court providing to the aggrieved spouse, the exclusive right of residence in the house which may belong to the victim or the perpetrator or to both parties. An occupation order cannot be sought against a party who does not reside under the same roof even if the latter is the owner of the residence.

    What is a Tenancy Order ?

    Where the place of residence is rented and is the site where violence is inflicted, the court may at the request of one of the spouses, victim of violence, order that the rented accommodation be conferred to the victim. This order may be promulgated if the perpetrator resides under the same roof and enjoys rights as tenant.

    What is an Interim Order ?

    The court can issue an Interim Protection Order, an Interim Occupation Order or an Interim Order in an event characterized by a significant risk of continued infliction of violence which may arise before the case is heard in the court. The interim orders are issued by the District Court on the same day the request is formulated.

    If the prepetrator doesn`t respect the court order ?

    Where an individual fails to respect an interim, tenancy or an Ancillary order of the court, he is liable to a fine not exceeding Rs 25,000 or to imprisonment for a period not exceeding 2 years. Please call the police station closest to your place of residence whenever the perpetrator refuses to adhere to the court order.

    Right of Appeal under the Protection from Domestic Violence Act !

    The law provides for a right of appeal in connection with the protection, occupation or tenancy order in front of the judge in Chambers. No right of appeal shall lie against an Interim Protection, Interim Occupation, or Interim Tenancy orders.

Family Welfare & Protection Officers

Who are the Family Welfare and Protection officers and how can they help the victims?

You can obtain the help and support you need by contacting one of the Family Welfare and Protection officers of the Ministry of Womenâ€™s Rights, Child Development and Family Welfare through the FSB closest to your place of residence. The Family Welfare and Protection officers may help you by:

  • Providing you with information on your rights to protection against domestic violence.
  • Taking you to a place of safety.
  • Assisting you in seeking treatment at hospital.
  • Helping in lodging your complaint at District Court level.
  • Where necessary, to accompany you to collect your personal belongings at your place of residence.
  • With your permission, the Family Welfare and Protection Officers can file a request on your behalf for a protection, occupation or tenancy order.

Who benefits from a protetcion ?

Who benefits from a protection under the Protection from Domestic Violence?

  • Spouses
  • Whether civilly or religiously married to each other.
  • Living or having lived together as husband and wife.
  • Having lived together or not and who are the parents of the same child
  • A child of one of the spouses whether he/she is:
  • Biological
  • Adopted
  • Placed in adoption (that is in the process of being adopted)
  • Step-daughter or step-son
  • Under the custody and care of one spouse
  • Any person living under the same roof.
